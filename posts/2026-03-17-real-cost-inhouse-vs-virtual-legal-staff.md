When law firms evaluate staffing options, they usually compare hourly rates. A virtual assistant at $8/hr sounds cheap. An in-house hire at $22/hr sounds reasonable. But hourly rates are only one part of the equation — and often the smallest part.

Here's a complete cost comparison that most firms never do.

## The True Cost of an In-House Legal Hire

Let's use a legal assistant as the example. The average salary for an in-house legal assistant in the U.S. is around $45,000/year. But your actual cost as an employer is significantly higher:

| Cost Item | Annual Estimate |
|---|---|
| Base salary | $45,000 |
| Payroll taxes (FICA ~7.65%) | $3,443 |
| Health insurance contribution | $6,000–$8,000 |
| Paid time off (15 days) | $2,596 |
| Recruiting / hiring costs | $3,000–$5,000 |
| Training and onboarding | $1,500–$3,000 |
| Equipment and software | $1,500–$2,500 |
| Office space (per desk) | $3,000–$8,000 |
| **Total** | **$66,000–$76,500** |

That works out to **$32–$37/hr** for a role that shows up on the books at $22/hr.
