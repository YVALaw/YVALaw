In a personal injury law firm, the demand package is one of the most important documents in a case. It's the formal communication to an insurance adjuster laying out the facts, injuries, damages, and the settlement amount your client deserves. Done well, it accelerates settlement. Done poorly, it invites lowball offers and unnecessary delays.

Yet in many firms, demand writing falls to whoever has time — which usually means a paralegal already stretched thin or an attorney who'd rather be in court.

## What Demand Writing Actually Involves

A complete demand package typically includes:

- **Case summary** — facts of the incident, liability narrative, supporting evidence
- **Medical record review** — organizing and summarizing treatment records, physician notes, and diagnostic results
- **Medical bill compilation** — itemizing all treatment costs with supporting documentation
- **Damages calculation** — medical specials, lost wages, future treatment estimates, pain and suffering
- **Demand letter** — a professional, persuasive letter addressed to the adjuster presenting the full claim
- **Supporting exhibits** — photos, police reports, wage verification, expert opinions

A thorough demand package for a moderate PI case can take 8–15 hours to prepare. For a firm handling 50–100 active cases, that's a significant time burden.

## Why a Generalist Struggles With Demand Writing

Demand writing is a specialized skill. It requires:

- The ability to read and interpret medical records accurately
- Knowledge of how damages are calculated and presented
- Familiarity with the standards insurers use to evaluate claims
- Strong legal writing ability — clear, precise, and persuasive
- Attention to detail that avoids errors that undercut your settlement position

A paralegal who also manages calendars, handles client calls, and files documents rarely has the sustained focus demand writing requires. The result is demand packages that go out late, incomplete, or below the standard that gets results.

## What a Demand Writing Specialist Does Differently

A dedicated demand writer focuses exclusively on building demand packages. Their workflow is streamlined:

1. Receive the case file and medical records
2. Review and summarize all records and bills
3. Calculate damages using the firm's established methodology
4. Draft the demand letter to the firm's standards and style
5. Compile and format the full package for attorney review
6. Make revisions based on attorney feedback
7. Handle adjuster follow-up correspondence

Because they do this all day, every day, they're faster, more accurate, and more consistent than a generalist handling it as a secondary task.
