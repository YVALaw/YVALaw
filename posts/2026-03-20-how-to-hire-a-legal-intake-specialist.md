Every missed call is a missed case. For law firms — especially personal injury and employment practices — the intake process is where potential clients are won or lost. Yet most firms either understaff this function or leave it to whoever happens to be available.

A dedicated legal intake specialist changes that. Here's everything you need to know about hiring one.

## What Does a Legal Intake Specialist Actually Do?

A legal intake specialist is the first human contact a potential client has with your firm. Their job is to:

- Answer incoming calls and web inquiries promptly
- Screen leads for eligibility and case viability
- Capture complete intake forms with accurate information
- Enter data into your CRM or case management software
- Schedule consultations with the right attorney
- Follow up on leads that didn't convert immediately

Done well, intake is a sales and service function. Done poorly, it's a revenue leak.

## The Case for a Dedicated Intake Role

Many firms assign intake duties to a receptionist, paralegal, or whoever picks up the phone. This creates three problems:

**1. Inconsistency.** Intake quality varies depending on who answers. A paralegal juggling document prep will rush the call. A receptionist may not know which questions to ask.

**2. Missed follow-up.** Studies consistently show that law firms fail to follow up with 40–60% of inbound leads. A dedicated intake specialist owns follow-up as a core responsibility.

**3. Lost bilingual leads.** If your intake team can't communicate with Spanish-speaking callers, you're leaving a significant portion of the market untouched.

## What to Look For When Hiring

Whether you hire in-house or virtually, a strong legal intake specialist should have:

- **Excellent communication skills** — clear, warm, and professional on every call
- **Bilingual fluency** (English/Spanish) — essential for firms serving diverse markets
- **Familiarity with CRM tools** — Clio, Filevine, Salesforce, or similar
- **Attention to detail** — intake errors downstream cause real problems
- **Empathy** — clients reaching out to a law firm are often in a stressful situation
